I started programming in high school when I found an old book on Qbasic on my dads book shelf. It was very simple but, the thrill of getting a computer to do what ever I wanted it to grabbed me and would not let go.

After high school I followed my love of programming to UTA where I studied computer engineering for three years. C and C++ were a revelation after the simple assembly programming of Qbasic. I quickly grew to love the flexibility of object oriented programming and dug into it.

After 3 years I realized I didn’t care for most of the engineering but still enjoyed the programming. I left UTA and found my way to “The Art Institute.” I found that web development let me use my talent for programming without having to worry about how the hard ware was designed.
